Introduction/Background The proposed project spotlights Yiddish, a language with a storied history within Ashkenazi Jewish communities across Europe and America. Once spoken by millions, Yiddish has seen a significant decline since the Second World War, facing challenges from the Holocaust, assimilation, and shifts to dominant languages such as English, Hebrew, and Russian. Despite this, there is a resurgent interest in Yiddish culture and language, with educational programs, literature, theater, and digital media contributing to its revitalization. This project intends to document the current state of Yiddish; explore its historical significance, cultural richness, and the socio-political factors influencing its vitality; and propose comprehensive strategies for its preservation and growth.
